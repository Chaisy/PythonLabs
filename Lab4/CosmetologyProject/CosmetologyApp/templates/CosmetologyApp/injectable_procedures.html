Injectable procedures

Injectables and Botox are widely used in the field of cosmetology to address various aesthetic concerns and promote a more youthful appearance. Let’s take a closer look at each of them: Injectables: Injectables refer to a broad category of cosmetic treatments that involve the injection of substances into the skin to improve its appearance. There are two primary types of injectables: dermal fillers and neuromodulators.

  • Dermal fillers: These are substances injected into the skin to add volume, fill in wrinkles, and enhance facial contours. They are commonly used to address signs of aging, such as fine lines, wrinkles, and loss of facial volume. Dermal fillers are made from different materials, including hyaluronic acid, calcium hydroxylapatite, and poly-L-lactic acid. The effects of dermal fillers can last anywhere from several months to over a year, depending on the type used.
  • Neuromodulators: Neuromodulators, such as Botox, Dysport, and Xeomin, are substances injected into specific facial muscles to temporarily block nerve signals, thereby relaxing the muscles and reducing the appearance of wrinkles and fine lines. Botox, in particular, is widely recognized and used for this purpose. The effects of neuromodulators typically last around three to six months.

Botox: Botox is the brand name for a type of botulinum toxin produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum. It has medical and cosmetic applications, but in cosmetology, it is primarily used for facial rejuvenation. It’s crucial to remember that injectables and Botox are medical procedures and should only be administered by qualified and trained medical professionals, such as dermatologists or plastic surgeons. Improper administration can lead to adverse effects and complications. Patients should have a consultation with a qualified practitioner to discuss their goals and determine the most suitable treatment plan for their specific needs.
